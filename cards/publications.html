Hidden Voices: Reducing gender data gap, one Wikipedia article at a time

May 2023

WikiWorkshop 2023

  • A social initiative aimed at diminishing gender bias on the internet by creating Wikipedia biographies for women with limited online representation.
  • Developed a comprehensive pipeline to build a knowledge graph by systematically scraping and ranking web data related to individuals of interest.
  • Conducted rigorous testing of various NLP techniques, both traditional and modern, to identify the most effective method for extracting information.
  • Explored various approaches to mitigate hallucinations in the biographies produced by language models.
  • Spearheaded a data collection hackathon on our platform, resulting in a number of contributions of biographies for noteworthy women.

Segmented ε-Greedy for Solving a Redesigned Multi-arm Bandit Environment

Sep 2022

Springer Conference

  • Developed a deep reinforcement learning agent using a segmentation approach for efficient policy learning in visually-changing environments (e.g., Super Mario Bros. with multiple levels).
  • Implemented a novel policy that segments the environment, enabling the agent to rapidly adapt and relearn within new segments, leading to significant performance improvements over vanilla ε-Greedy DQN in the Mario environment.
  • To address computational limitations, redesigned the Multi-Armed Bandit problem to create a Mario-like environment for efficient agent training.

Early prediction of sepsis using machine learning

Jan 2021

IEEE Conference

  • Built a high-accuracy machine learning model (98.88% accuracy, 99.87% AUROC) using LGBM classifier to predict sepsis in critically ill patients up to 6 hours before clinical diagnosis.
  • Leveraged a large ICU patient dataset (>40,000) to train the model, potentially enabling earlier interventions and improved patient outcomes.
  • Employed LIME for explainable AI, facilitating healthcare professionals' understanding and trust in the model's predictions.

Detection of covid-19 using chest x-ray scans

Oct 2020

IEEE Conference - Best Paper Award

  • Led the development of a deep learning-based computer vision system for COVID-19 detection from chest X-ray scans (awarded Best Paper under track Technologies and Medical Innovations related to COVID-19).
  • Performed a comprehensive analysis of various deep learning models for accurate two-way and three-way classification of COVID-19 cases.
